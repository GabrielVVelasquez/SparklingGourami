Species Snapshot

Species Name: Trichopsis pumila

Common Names: Sparkling Gourami, Pygmy Gourami

Lifespan: 3+ years

Origin: Southeast Asia

Also known as the Pygmy Gourami, the Sparkling Gourami is one of the smallest labyrinth fish out there. Growing to an average size of one and a half inches, to the untrained eye, these rare and exotic fish can easily be mistakenly misidentified as a juvenile or female variant of their more widely known cousin, Betta splendens, otherwise known as the betta.

However, unlike their betta brethren, the Sparkling Gourami posses a surprising ability to produce what can be described as a croaking sound, an ability similar to that of another one of the Sparkling Gourami's slightly larger, although less colorful cousins, Trichopsis vittata, otherwise known as the Croaking Gourami. Although the Sparkling Gourami's croak isn't loud enough to be heard all the way across one's house, its croak is loud enough to be heard by one who is in the same room as the tank the fish is situated in.

The name "Sparkling Gourami" came to be due to the fish's iridescent scales which appear to "sparkle" under proper lighting. And although they may come off as shy and skittish when mistakenly kept in small numbers, when kept in large enough groups, these intelligent fish can prove to be a fun and worthwhile addition to the aquarium due to their unique behaviors and diverse personalities.
