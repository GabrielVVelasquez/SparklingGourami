Tank Size

A group of five or six Sparkling Gouramis could be kept in a tank as small as 10 to 15 gallons, however a larger aquarium would be recommended as the fish would have more room to swim in and larger tanks are less likely to have drastic fluctuations in their water parameters. To comfortably house a group of Sparkling Gouramis, an established aquarium of 20 gallons or more would be recommended.

Water Parameters

Although considered to be generally hardy, Sparkling Gouramis still appreciate and thrive in clean and stable conditions. As with all fish, having unstable water parameters could lead to sickness and diseases, reduced lifespan, and even death. As like other gouramis, these fish don't do well with high flow and would instead prefer a low to moderate amount of flow in their aquariums.

Ideal tank parameters for Sparkling Gouramis:

Water Temperature: 77-83°F (25-28.3°C)

pH Level: 6.0-7.5

General Hardness: 5-19 dGH

Carbonate Hardness: 4-8 dKH

While Sparkling Gouramis don't technically require highly oxygenated water due to their labyrinth organs, these fish will still appreciate increased levels of oxygen in their water. As with all other fish, make sure to perform water changes as needed to keep your aquarium's ammonia and nitrite levels at bay.

Tank Décor

Because of their lighter colors, Sparkling Gouramis will appear more colorful and vibrant against a darker substrate. To properly enjoy this species adequate lighting is required, as without proper lighting these fish's reflective scales will appear dull and unimpressive. Also, if you're going to have live plants in your aquarium, adequate lighting as well as proper fertilization will be crucial for supporting your plants' growth.

An important feature to include in your tank for these fish would be the addition of hides/territories for each fish to be able to claim and retreat into, as although generally peaceful, these fish can become rather territorial with each other. Breaking up lines of sight would help in reducing aggression between males via driftwood, rockwork, plants, or other tank decor.
